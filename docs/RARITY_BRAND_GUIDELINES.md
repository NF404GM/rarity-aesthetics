## 2. LOGO USAGE & SPECIFICATIONS

### Primary Logo

**Description:**  
The Rarity Aesthetics logo features elegant script typography with "Rarity" in gold (#D4AF37) and "Aesthetics" in navy blue serif font. The design conveys luxury, artistry, and professionalism.

**Logo Variations Required:**

1. **Primary Full-Color Logo**
   - Gold "Rarity" + Navy "Aesthetics"
   - Use on white/light backgrounds
   - Default for all applications

2. **White Logo**
   - All-white version
   - Use on dark backgrounds and photography
   - Maintain legibility

3. **Gold Monochrome**
   - All gold (#D4AF37)
   - For special luxury applications
   - Metallic print materials

4. **Navy Monochrome**
   - All navy (#1B3A52)
   - For professional documents
   - Black & white printing

5. **Icon/Mark**
   - "RA" monogram or simplified mark
   - For social media avatars
   - Small applications (favicons, app icons)

### Clear Space & Sizing

**Clear Space Rule:**  
Maintain clear space equal to the height of the letter "A" in "Aesthetics" around all sides of the logo. No text, graphics, or other elements should intrude into this space.

**Minimum Sizes:**
- Print: 1.5 inches wide minimum
- Digital: 180 pixels wide minimum
- Social media profile: 400 x 400 pixels minimum

**Maximum Sizes:**
- No maximum, but maintain proportions
- Ensure clarity at all sizes

### Logo Placement Guidelines

**Preferred Placements:**
- Top left corner (websites, documents)
- Centered (packaging, marketing materials)
- Bottom right corner (photography watermark)

**Never:**
- Place on busy backgrounds where legibility is compromised
- Distort, rotate, or skew the logo
- Change colors outside of approved variations
- Add effects (drop shadows, gradients, outlines)
- Rearrange elements
- Use outdated logo versions

### File Formats

**Required Formats:**
- .AI (Adobe Illustrator - master file)
- .EPS (vector, for print)
- .SVG (vector, for web)
- .PNG (transparent background, for digital)
- .JPG (with white background, for email)

**Color Modes:**
- RGB for digital applications
- CMYK for print applications
- Pantone for specialty printing

## 4. TYPOGRAPHY SYSTEM

### Brand Typefaces

**Display Font (Logo & Headers)**

**Rarity (Script):**
- Current logo script font
- Use: Logo only, special headers (sparingly)
- Never use for body text or paragraphs
- Evokes: Elegance, artistry, luxury

### Primary Typeface (Headings)

**Montserrat OR Playfair Display**

**Montserrat:**
- Weight: Bold (700) for H1-H2
- Weight: SemiBold (600) for H3-H4
- Use: Modern, clean headers
- Character: Contemporary, approachable luxury
- Best for: Website, digital materials

**Playfair Display:**
- Weight: Bold (700) for H1-H2
- Weight: SemiBold (600) for H3-H4
- Use: Traditional, elegant headers
- Character: Classic, sophisticated luxury
- Best for: Print materials, formal documents

**Choose ONE consistently within each project/medium**

### Secondary Typeface (Body & UI)

**Lato**
- Weight: Regular (400) for body text
- Weight: Bold (700) for emphasis
- Use: All body text, captions, UI elements
- Character: Clean, readable, professional
- Line height: 1.6 for optimal readability

### Type Hierarchy

**H1 - Main Headlines**
- Font: Montserrat/Playfair Display Bold
- Size: 36-48pt (print) | 36-48px (web)
- Color: Navy (#1B3A52)
- Usage: Page titles, major sections

**H2 - Section Headers**
- Font: Montserrat/Playfair Display Bold
- Size: 28-32pt (print) | 28-32px (web)
- Color: Navy (#1B3A52)
- Usage: Content sections, subsections

**H3 - Subsection Headers**
- Font: Montserrat/Playfair Display SemiBold OR Lato Bold
- Size: 20-24pt (print) | 20-24px (web)
- Color: Navy (#1B3A52)
- Usage: Smaller divisions, card headers

**H4 - Minor Headers**
- Font: Lato Bold
- Size: 16-18pt (print) | 16-18px (web)
- Color: Navy (#1B3A52)
- Usage: List headers, small sections

**Body Text**
- Font: Lato Regular
- Size: 11-12pt (print) | 16px (web)
- Color: Navy (#1B3A52) or dark gray (#4A4A4A)
- Line height: 1.6
- Usage: Paragraphs, descriptions

**Captions & Small Text**
- Font: Lato Regular
- Size: 9-10pt (print) | 14px (web)
- Color: Gray (#4A4A4A)
- Usage: Image captions, disclaimers, footnotes

### Typography Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Use generous white space
- Maintain hierarchy (don't skip levels)
- Align text left for body copy
- Use sentence case for most headers
- Ensure sufficient contrast with backgrounds

**DON'T:**
- Mix more than 2 font families in one piece
- Use all caps for long passages (short headers OK)
- Use center-align for body text
- Use justified alignment (creates uneven spacing)
- Stack multiple bold elements (reduce hierarchy)
- Use drop shadows or effects on text

## 5. PHOTOGRAPHY GUIDELINES

### Visual Style

**Overall Aesthetic:**
- Clean, light, airy
- Professional yet approachable
- Soft, flattering lighting
- Minimal backgrounds (avoid clutter)
- Focus on eyes and lashes
- Natural skin tones (avoid over-filtering)

### Photography Categories

**1. Lash Work (Portfolio)**

**Close-Up Lash Shots:**
- Focus: Crystal-clear lashes, sharp detail
- Angle: 45-degree angle showing eye and lashes
- Lighting: Soft, diffused (avoid harsh shadows)
- Background: Blurred, neutral
- Editing: Enhance lashes, natural skin tone
- Must show: Before AND after for transformations

**Before & After:**
- Same angle, same lighting for both shots
- Clear "before" label and "after" label
- Side-by-side or split-screen layout
- Show dramatic transformation
- Client eyes open (not closed during work)

**2. Behind the Scenes**

**Studio Space:**
- Clean, organized lash station
- Branded materials visible (subtle)
- Good lighting showcasing professionalism
- Products arranged aesthetically
- No visible clutter

**Process Shots:**
- Ashley working (hands, tools, precision)
- Product organization
- Lash mapping process
- Client experience (comfortable, relaxed)
- Professional setup

**3. Product Photography**

**Individual Products:**
- Clean white background OR lifestyle setting
- Multiple angles (front, back, close-up)
- Props: Minimal, complementary
- Lighting: Bright, even, no harsh shadows
- Styling: Elegant, luxury feel

**Product in Use:**
- Show products being used/held
- Natural hand positioning
- Focus on product with soft background blur
- Brand labels visible but not forced

**4. Lifestyle & Brand**

**Ashley Portrait:**
- Professional headshots
- Working shots (natural, candid)
- Mix of posed and candid
- Consistent editing style
- Wardrobe: Professional, on-brand colors

**Client Experience:**
- Comfortable lash bed
- Relaxed client (with permission/release)
- Final reveal moments (joy, confidence)
- Diverse representation (various ages, skin tones)

### Photography Specifications

**Technical Requirements:**
- **Resolution:** Minimum 300 DPI for print, 72 DPI for web
- **Format:** RAW for shooting, JPG/PNG for delivery
- **Dimensions:** 
  - Social media: 1080 x 1080px (square), 1080 x 1350px (portrait)
  - Website hero: 1920 x 1080px minimum
  - Blog images: 1200px wide
- **File size:** Under 200KB after optimization for web

**Editing Guidelines:**
- Consistent color grading (light, airy, slightly warm)
- Natural skin tones (avoid heavy filters)
- Enhance lashes (clarity, sharpness)
- Remove distractions (clone tool for blemishes only)
- Maintain authenticity (don't completely alter reality)
- Add logo watermark on portfolio pieces (subtle, bottom right corner)

### Image Don'ts

**NEVER:**
- Use low-resolution, pixelated images
- Use stock photos of lashes (only real Rarity work)
- Over-filter or heavily photoshop client photos
- Include unflattering angles
- Show messy or unprofessional backgrounds
- Use images without proper rights/permissions
- Post client photos without written consent

## 6. VOICE & TONE

### Brand Voice (Always)

The Rarity Aesthetics voice is:

**Warm & Approachable**
- Write like talking to a knowledgeable friend
- Avoid clinical, cold language
- Use "you" to speak directly to reader
- Conversational but professional

**Confident & Expert**
- Demonstrate knowledge without being condescending
- Use specific terminology when appropriate
- Back up claims with experience/credentials
- Own your expertise: "I specialize in..." not "I try to..."

**Luxury-Focused**
- Emphasize premium quality, custom experience
- Use elevated vocabulary (but not pretentious)
- Focus on transformation and artistry
- Highlight exclusivity and attention to detail

**Educational & Helpful**
- Provide value in every communication
- Explain "why" not just "what"
- Empower clients with knowledge
- Offer tips, tricks, insights

**Authentic & Transparent**
- Be honest about processes, timing, results
- Share real stories and experiences
- Admit limitations when appropriate
- Maintain Ashley's personality and passion

### Tone (Adapts to Context)

**On Social Media: Friendly & Inspiring**
- Upbeat, positive, engaging
- Use emojis sparingly (1-3 per caption)
- Ask questions, encourage interaction
- Celebrate clients and their beauty

**On Website: Professional & Informative**
- Clear, concise, helpful
- Focus on client benefits
- Structured, scannable content
- CTAs are direct and actionable

**In Email: Personal & Valuable**
- Personalize with first names
- Conversational subject lines
- Provide real value (not just sales)
- Warm sign-offs (e.g., "Can't wait to see you!")

**In Client Communications: Caring & Clear**
- Empathetic and understanding
- Set clear expectations
- Provide detailed instructions
- Reassure and build confidence

### Voice Examples

**‚ùå Wrong Tone (Too Clinical):**
"We provide lash extension application services utilizing premium synthetic fibers applied with medical-grade adhesive."

**‚úÖ Right Tone (Warm, Expert, Luxury):**
"Every lash set is a custom work of art, designed specifically for your unique eye shape using premium, ultra-lightweight extensions that look and feel completely natural."

**‚ùå Wrong Tone (Too Casual):**
"OMG your lashes are gonna look sooo good! üòçüî•üíï Book now babe!"

**‚úÖ Right Tone (Friendly but Professional):**
"Ready to wake up with effortlessly beautiful lashes? ‚ú® Let's create your perfect custom set. Book your appointment today!"

### Writing Guidelines

**DO:**
- Start with benefits, not features
- Use active voice ("I create custom lashes" not "Custom lashes are created")
- Keep sentences concise (aim for 15-20 words average)
- Use contractions for warmth (you're, we're, it's)
- Tell stories (client transformations, Ashley's journey)
- Include calls-to-action (clear next steps)

**DON'T:**
- Use jargon without explanation
- Write in all caps (except brief emphasis)
- Over-use exclamation points (max 1 per paragraph)
- Make unsubstantiated claims ("world's best")
- Use negative language about competitors
- Write overly long paragraphs (3-5 sentences max)
